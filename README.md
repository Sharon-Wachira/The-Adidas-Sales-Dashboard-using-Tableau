# Adidas Sales Analysis Dashboard 

## Project Overview

This project presents a *Power BI-driven sales analysis dashboard* for Adidas, designed to deliver actionable insights into sales performance, profitability, and market dynamics.

The analysis covers both:

* **Overall business performance**
* **Deep-dive into low-performing product segments**

The objective is to support **data-driven decision-making** and highlight both **opportunities and risks** within the business.

## Business Problem

Adidas operates in a highly competitive sportswear market and requires clear visibility into:

* Sales drivers
* Product performance
* Regional demand
* Retailer contribution

Without structured insights, the business risks:

* Over-reliance on specific products or retailers
* Missing growth opportunities
* Inefficient pricing and inventory strategies


## Business Objectives

* Understand **sales dynamics and performance drivers**
* Identify **high and low-performing regions**
* Analyze **product-level performance**
* Evaluate **pricing and profitability**
* Provide **actionable recommendations for growth**



## Key Performance Indicators (Overall)

* **Total Sales:** $900M
* **Operating Profit:** $332M
* **Units Sold:** 2M
* **Avg Price per Unit:** $45
* **Operating Margin:** 42%

## Dashboard Features

* **Sales by Month (Area Chart)** → Trend & seasonality
* **Sales by State (Map)** → Geographic distribution
* **Sales by Region (Treemap)** → Regional contribution
* **Sales by Product (Bar Chart)** → Product performance
* **Sales by Retailer (Bar Chart)** → Retailer contribution

---

## Insights from Filtered Analysis

###  1. High Margin Despite Low Sales

* Margin remains **42%**

 **Conclusion:**

* Problem is **not profitability**, but **low demand**


### 2. Demand Issue (Not Pricing)

* Lower sales volume with slightly **higher price ($46)**

 **Insight:**

* Pricing may be slightly restrictive
* Weak perceived value or demand

---

###  3. Seasonal Trends Still Apply

* Peaks around **July–August**
* Drops around **March & October**

 **Insight:**

* Even weak products follow **market seasonality**


###  4. Same Regional Dependence

* West still leads (~30%)

 **Insight:**

* Demand exists but is **geographically uneven**

---

### 5. Retailer Concentration Persists

* West Gear & Foot Locker still dominate

 **Risk:**

* Weak products are also **over-dependent on few retailers**

---

###  6. Critical Underperformance in Women’s Segment

* Women’s Athletic Footwear contributes **very little**

 **Insight:**

* Major **missed market opportunity**
* Possible issues:

  * Product-market fit
  * Branding
  * Competition


##  Key Problems Identified

* Low demand for certain products
* Weak product positioning
* Over-reliance on limited retailers
* Underperformance in women’s category


## Business Recommendations

###  1. Product Diversification

* Reduce reliance on **Men’s Street Footwear**
* Strengthen underperforming categories


### 2. Improve Product Positioning

* Rebrand and reposition:

  * Women’s Athletic Footwear
  * Men’s Apparel

---

### 3. Optimize Pricing Strategy

* Test:

  * Discounts
  * Bundling
  * Seasonal promotions

---

###  4. Expand Retail Channels

* Reduce dependency on:

  * West Gear
  * Foot Locker

* Expand into:

  * E-commerce
  * New retail partnerships


###  5. Regional Growth Strategy

* Invest in underperforming regions
* Use localized campaigns

---

###  6. Focus on Women’s Market

* Redesign and market women’s products
* Tap into **athleisure and fitness trends**


## Final Conclusion

This project highlights that:

 **High performance does not eliminate risk**
 **Low-performing products are not failing — they are underutilized**

Adidas has the opportunity to:

* Diversify revenue streams
* Reduce dependency risks
* Unlock growth in untapped segments
